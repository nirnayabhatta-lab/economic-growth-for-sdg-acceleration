# Framework Note

The platform organizes analysis around one question:

How can countries, with Nepal as the anchor case, generate inclusive and resilient growth that accelerates SDG delivery?

The analytical logic has four layers:
- growth engine
- transmission channels
- SDG outcomes
- policy levers

The platform is designed to make direct and indirect SDG support visible:
- direct support: policies or spending explicitly target SDG outcomes
- indirect support: growth, productivity, financing, or resilience actions support SDGs through jobs, fiscal space, or service capacity

The distinctive analytical lens is a three-speed model:
- annual structural position
- weekly or near-term acceleration pulse
- policy and intervention leverage

This is intended to make the platform useful not just for reporting performance, but for understanding motion:
- what is strengthening momentum
- what is dragging momentum
- what interventions could change the trajectory fastest

The seven core lenses are:
- macroeconomic structure, productivity, and diversification
- employment, informality, and quality jobs
- gender, LNOB, and territorial inequality
- LDC graduation and smooth transition risk
- SDG financing, fiscal space, and private capital
- climate, disaster risk, and resilience
- governance, coordination, and implementation capacity
